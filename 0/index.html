Part 1: Selfie — Wrong Way vs. Right Way

Close-up distorted selfie

Zoomed-in corrected selfie

Reflection

I realized that close-up selfies look warped. If you want to take a good selfie, it's important to step back and zoom in so that the selfie looks more natural. The reason why is that when the camera is close, the angles formed are wider. Closer parts (like the nose) project much larger on the sensor than farther parts (like the ears). When I stepped back, the rays from my face were closer to parallel, so the proportions look more realistic.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Zoomed-in from further (compressed)

Wide-angle from closer (expanded)

Reflection

When zooming in from farther away, I noticed that the path and trees looked more compressed together and it was harder to see depth. This phenomenon is happening because when zooming in, I stand farther back, so depth differences matter less. With a wide focal length up close, differences in depth get dramaticized even further, making the foreground take up most of the image and the background become much smaller
